Trello Time Tracking

Hey, I'm with Timepeeking, a time-tracking and invoicing service. I’m writing this to help you make a decision on which time-tracking service to use in conjunction with Trello. Maybe I can sell you on Timepeeking, but I also suggest two alternatives for folks depending on their needs.

The three alternatives I discuss here are Harvest, Plus for Trello, and Timepeeking.

Please note that this site is not affiliated with or endorsed by the maker of Trello.

The Alternatives

Harvest

Harvest is a well known time-tracking and invoicing service with a large user base. They have produced a Google Chrome integration that allows you to:

  • create time entries without leaving Trello
  • see the time tracked to each card from the card itself
  • follow links in your Harvest timesheet to individual Trello cards
  • view reports regarding the time you've spent

By tracking your time with Harvest from Trello, you can view reports analyzing that time and send invoices. It appears from the Harvest sales page for the Trello integration that the integration has not been improved upon in any substantial way since its introduction nearly two and a half years ago. I’m not a Harvest customer (imagine that) and can’t comment on functionality beyond what I’ve seen published by Harvest.

Plus for Trello

Plus for Trello is a free, well-rated Chrome integration. An Android app is also available and an iOS app is planned. Plus for Trello allows the user to:

  • create time entries without leaving Trello
  • see the time tracked to each card from the card itself
  • create estimates of the time it will take to finish the Trello card
  • view reports regarding the time you've spent

The mobile app is limited in functionality. It lets you view information and offers links that bring up the Trello app but doesn’t let you enter time or card time estimates.

Timepeeking

Timepeeking, which I work on, is a web-only time-tracking and invoicing service. Integration with Trello includes a Chrome extension and the timepeeking.com website. Our features allow the user to:

  • create time entries without leaving Trello
  • see the time tracked to each card from the card itself
  • follow links from the time entry search page to the Trello cards, etc.
  • create cards, lists, boards, and organizations without having to visit trello.com

Timepeeking also lets you attribute time to cards from timepeeking.com, that is, without having to visit trello.com. Timepeeking is the only Trello time-tracking integration I’ve seen that allows the user to create Trello cards outside of Trello.

Help in Choosing

If cost is your primary concern in choosing an integration, Plus for Trello will be right for you because it’s free. Harvest has a free plan, but it’s very limited in the number of clients and projects you can have.

Though Harvest, Plus for Trello, and Timepeeking all provide charts showing progess toward a time estimate, Plus for Trello is the only one I’ve seen documentation for that shows data in charts at the level of the Trello card (or list, or board). Harvest provides (at least) charts showing progress on a month-to-month basis, though it appears that these data are shown regardless of the source, Trello or otherwise. Timepeeking shows only the total amount worked toward an estimate, and, again, without regard to the source.

If you need to invoice clients and want the assurance that a larger company provides, Harvest probably provides the most sensible solution. If you need to invoice clients and Trello is a tool that you use frequently, Timepeeking (again, where I work) will probably be the fastest tool to use because it frees you from having to click on Trello cards to track time, and because cards, lists, boards, and organizations can be created without having to visit Trello.
